Freelancer Money Tips
01

Pay yourself a "salary"

Move a fixed amount to a personal account each month regardless of what clients pay when — smooths the feast-and-famine cycle.

02

Separate tax money on arrival

The moment a payment lands, shift your set-aside percentage to a separate account so it's never spent by accident.

03

Buffer before big bets

A 3–6 month buffer is what lets you say no to bad-fit clients and negotiate from strength.

04

Track by client, not just total

Knowing your income concentration reveals hidden risk long before a client actually leaves.

05

Review subscriptions quarterly

Tools accumulate fast for freelancers — a quarterly audit usually finds 10–20% in easy savings.

06

Invoice with net-15, not net-30

Shorter payment terms reduce the cash-flow gap that forces reliance on the buffer.

Getting-Started Checklist
Resources
  • Keep a rolling 12-month income/expense log outside any single client relationship.
  • Learn your local self-employment tax filing calendar and set calendar reminders a week ahead of each due date.
  • Open a dedicated business/tax savings account so freelance money never fully mixes with personal spending.
  • Revisit your day-rate or project pricing at least twice a year against actual hours logged.
