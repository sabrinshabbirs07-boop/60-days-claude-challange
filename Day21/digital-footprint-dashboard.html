09 · Summary

Final Verdict

Across 15 services spanning 11 parent companies, this stack covers nearly every major personal-data category — social, financial, location, behavioral, and biometric. That breadth is what drives a Significant (78/100) Digital Footprint Score.

Roughly a quarter of the stack sits inside a single company (Alphabet/Google), with another chunk inside Meta — meaning two corporate ecosystems can see a disproportionate share of activity. Combined with few visible privacy-hardening habits, that produces a Fair (34/100) Privacy Score.

None of this reflects actual account data, real permissions, or verified identity — it's a structural read of the app list itself, intended to highlight where attention is best spent.

1

Turn off ad personalization across Google and Meta — the single biggest lever in the simulator above.

2

Audit location permissions on Snapchat, TikTok and the Google app suite; set to "while using" at most.

3

Add two-factor authentication to financial touchpoints — Google Pay and Amazon — before anything else.
