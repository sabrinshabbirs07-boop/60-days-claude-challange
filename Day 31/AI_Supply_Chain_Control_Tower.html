How the Control Tower works

A quick briefing before you take the seat.

Your goal

Keep four performance KPIs (Service Level, Customer Satisfaction, Inventory Health, Transport Efficiency) as high as possible, keep Operating Cost variance low, and protect as much revenue as you can — all before the 3-minute shift ends.

Alerts

Disruptions arrive continuously and get more frequent as the shift goes on. Each has a priority (Critical, High, Medium) and its own countdown — if it expires with no decision, that's treated as the worst possible outcome.

Actions

  • Contextual actions (e.g. Reroute Trucks, Use Backup Supplier) — one is usually the best fix, others are partial or mismatched fixes.
  • Ignore — resolves the alert immediately with a real penalty. Sometimes still better than a badly mismatched action, but never better than the right one.
  • Delay Decision — buys a little time but shortens the alert's remaining clock and adds a small cost. Can only be used twice per alert.

Feedback

After every decision you'll get a short toast explaining whether it was the right call and why — use it to calibrate your next move.

Grading

Your final grade blends average KPI health, decision accuracy, and total score into a letter from D to A+.
