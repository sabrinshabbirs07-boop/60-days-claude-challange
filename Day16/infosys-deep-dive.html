Business Overview

Infosys is India's second-largest IT services company (behind TCS), delivering consulting, technology, outsourcing, and digital transformation services globally. Its key verticals include Financial Services, Manufacturing, Retail, Communication, Energy & Utilities, and Hi-Tech. Infosys has a rapidly growing AI practice under CEO Salil Parekh, with a strategic collaboration with OpenAI announced in April 2026. The company employs ~337,000 people and serves clients across North America (~60% revenue), Europe (~25%), and rest of world.

Moat: Infosys benefits from strong client stickiness, large deal momentum, enterprise AI capabilities, and brand trust across blue-chip global clients.

Ownership Analysis

Key observation: FIIs have been steadily reducing exposure (from 34% to 28% over 8 quarters), while DIIs have significantly increased from 35.6% to 43.2%. This shift suggests domestic institutional confidence (mutual funds, insurance, etc.) is rising even as foreign investors take profits or reduce overweight positions, possibly due to rupee/dollar concerns and global IT sector de-rating.

Promoter holding (14.38%) is structurally low because the Murthy family and other founders diluted over decades. No pledging is a positive signal.

Public holding has declined from ~17% to 13.5% over 3 years — retail is becoming a smaller slice as institutions dominate.

💪 Strengths

  • Exceptional Return Ratios: ROE 32.2% and ROCE 40.3% — among the best in Indian large-cap IT, sustained over 10+ years.
  • Record-Level FCF: FY25 delivered the highest-ever FCF in Infosys history (₹33,457 Cr); FY26 at ₹31,259 Cr remains robust.
  • Near-Debt-Free Balance Sheet: D/E of ~0.10x with ~96x interest coverage; exceptional financial strength.
  • Consistent Dividend: 3.56% yield with ~66–68% payout ratio; dependable income stream for investors.
  • AI-First Strategy: Strategic alliance with OpenAI (April 2026), CMMI AI Maturity Leadership, and strong large deal TCV wins ($3.8B in Q1 FY26) reflecting enterprise AI adoption.
  • Valuation at Multi-Year Lows: P/E of 16.2x is significantly below historical range of 20–32x and below large-cap IT sector average.
  • Growing DII Confidence: Domestic institutions have raised stake from 35.6% to 43.2% over 8 quarters — domestic money is accumulating.

⚠️ Watch-Points

  • Revenue Growth Slowing (3Y CAGR: 7%): Post-FY22 boom, growth decelerated sharply. FY26 guidance of 1–3% CC growth reflects continued macro caution from global clients.
  • FII Exodus: Foreign investors have reduced from 34% to 28% over 8 quarters — this overhang may limit near-term price recovery.
  • Stock Price Underperformance: -16% over 1 year; 5Y CAGR of -1% vs earnings growing at 9% — multiple compression reflects sector-wide concerns.
  • Low Promoter Holding (14.38%): While structurally explained by founding-era dilution, it's a below-average promoter alignment signal vs peers.
  • US Macro / Client Discretionary Spending: ~60% of revenue from North America; any US recession or tech-spending freeze could weigh heavily.
  • Margin Ceiling: OPM has been range-bound at 23–24% for 3+ years. There's limited headroom for significant margin expansion.
Key Metric to Track
  • Large Deal TCV wins each quarter — the best leading indicator of revenue visibility.
  • Revenue growth guidance revision — any upgrade from 1–3% CC signals macro recovery.
  • FII holding trend — reversal of FII selling would be a major sentiment catalyst.
  • Operating margin trajectory — any sustained move above 24% would signal the success of Project Maximus efficiency drive.
  • AI deal monetization — Watch for revenue contribution from AI-led deals in earnings commentary.

Fundamental Quality: Strong

Infosys is a fundamentally strong business — near-debt-free, generating record FCF, delivering sustained 30%+ ROE/ROCE, growing profits at 8–14%, and consistent with high dividend payouts. The business quality is not in question. The current concern is around near-term revenue growth pace (macro headwinds in the US IT market) and the stock's P/E multiple, which has compressed significantly and now sits near decade-lows. The combination of strong business metrics + low valuation + high dividend yield makes this a notably different risk-reward profile vs its own historical averages.
