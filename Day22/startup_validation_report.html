Executive Summary

Investment Thesis
The AI Career Intelligence Platform addresses a structurally underserved market — the 600M+ career-confused individuals in emerging economies — with a defensible, AI-native product at a moment when large language models make personalized mentorship economically viable for the first time.

This report evaluates an early-stage AI SaaS startup targeting students, fresh graduates, early-career professionals, and career switchers in India, with expansion ambitions across Southeast Asia and global emerging markets. The platform proposes to unify career mentoring, skill assessment, learning recommendations, productivity tracking, and career planning into a single, AI-powered ecosystem.

The core thesis is sound: fragmentation of career development tools is a genuine, high-frequency pain point with measurable demand, poor incumbent substitutes, and a large addressable market. The EdTech and HR technology markets in India alone are projected to reach $10B+ by 2028, and no single player has achieved dominant "career OS" positioning.

VALIDATE FURTHER. The opportunity is real and sizeable. However, investment readiness requires demonstrating: (a) measurable retention above 40% at 90 days, (b) willingness-to-pay evidence from ≥200 target users, and (c) a clear AI differentiation moat beyond API-wrapping incumbents.

Final Recommendation

VALIDATE FURTHER
Investment Committee Decision — June 2025

The committee finds this opportunity compelling in market size, problem severity, and long-term scalability. The AI career intelligence category is real, large, and early-innings in India. However, the startup is not yet investment-ready due to: (1) absence of traction data, (2) no demonstrated willingness-to-pay, (3) missing technical leadership, and (4) undefined AI differentiation beyond incumbents.

Recommendation: The founder should focus the next 90 days entirely on shipping a minimal, exceptional version of the AI career roadmap feature, acquiring 500 free users through organic content, and converting 50–100 to paid. These three data points — activation rate, retention, and conversion — will determine whether this moves to GO or NO-GO in the next evaluation cycle.

✅ GO Conditions
40%+ 30-day retention; 10%+ free-to-paid conversion; technical co-founder secured; B2B pilot signed
⚠️ VALIDATE FURTHER (Current)
Ship MVP; acquire 500 users; validate WTP; hire technical lead; gather quantitative market data
🚫 NO-GO Triggers
<20% 30-day retention; no willingness-to-pay evidence after 500 users; no technical co-founder by Month 4

2
Build & Ship (Days 8–14)

Launch waitlist landing page with a clear value prop and social proof
Begin publishing 3x/week content on LinkedIn (career roadmap insights; target audience problems)
Send MVP access to 50 hand-curated beta users; gather feedback within 48 hrs
3
Learn & Iterate (Days 15–21)
Analyze first-week retention data; identify and fix top 3 dropout points in onboarding
Run a "Superhero User" session: 5 power users; capture quotes and success stories
Cold email 10 Tier-2 engineering college placement officers with a free pilot offer
A/B test two onboarding flows: "goal-first" vs "skill-assessment-first"
Begin technical co-founder conversations; set hiring timeline
4
Grow & Decide (Days 22–30)
Reach 200 beta users; track 7-day and 14-day retention rates — target 45%+
Convert first 20 paid users at ₹299/mo using limited "Founding Member" offer
Compile 30-day data report: activation rate, retention, NPS, qualitative feedback themes
Present findings to 3 India angel investors / EdTech advisors for feedback
Make GO / NO-GO decision: commit to seed fundraise roadmap or pivot based on data
North Star Metric for Month 1
Target: 200 active beta users → 45%+ 14-day retention → 20 paid conversions. These three numbers, if achieved, create the foundation for a credible pre-seed fundraising narrative.
